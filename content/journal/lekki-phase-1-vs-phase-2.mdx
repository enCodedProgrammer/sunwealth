The easiest way to mispredict Lagos property returns is to treat "Lekki" as one market. It isn't. Lekki Phase 1 and Phase 2 differ on every axis that drives price: density, density trajectory, infrastructure age, tenant profile, and — crucially — how each responds to the Lekki–Epe corridor's continuing development.

**Phase 1** is the older, established quarter. Plots are smaller. Build quality across the estate is inconsistent because the estate developed over two decades; a 2006-era duplex next to a 2022-era maisonette tells you why unit economics can vary within a single street. Rental yields are moderate but the demand profile is stable — primarily Lagos-based professionals and families.

**Phase 2** is newer, larger, and still in net build-out. Plot sizes are larger. Infrastructure is more uniform but, depending on the specific cluster, less mature. Rental yields trail Phase 1 slightly but capital appreciation over a 5–10 year horizon has outpaced it in most recent cohorts — simply because the supply curve is still being drawn.

Which is better? It depends on what you're solving for:

- **Stable long-let income**: Phase 1.
- **Capital appreciation over a decade**: historically Phase 2, cluster-dependent.
- **Shortlet income**: Phase 1 edges out on occupancy, Phase 2 edges out on ADR for larger units.
- **A home for yourself**: whichever matches how you want to live. Price both in full, including service charge trajectory, before deciding.
